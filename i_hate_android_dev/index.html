This Is Why I Hate Android Development

11 July 2012

Well, this is the reason why I hate developing in android platform.

Introduction

Since my baby boy loves playing piano app on the ipad, I decide to make some piano app for ipad without annoying adv. This is what it looks like in the ipad.

Quiet simple, right?! Well, that's the vocal point :)

Unfortunately the app not available yet in the AppStore yet. But I promise you I will put it on AppStore, one day.

Since I also have a nice Samsung Galaxy Tab that runs IceCream Sandwich, then I decide to make the app avaliable for Android.

Enter Android World

Everything goes well for the beginning. And I love the new Eclipse (Juno), love how Android team improves ADT. But it's just not enough! I've always tested on the device. This is the look on GTab:

Nice! I like it! Not finished, I know, but nice!

The problem is when I choose to tried on emulator.

WOW! It perfectly fall apart! So if you want to make a good application on android, you must spent more time to design the UI to different device's screen size and dpi, and ofcourse, different android version.

See??! ADT gives us 15 different screen size and dpi! Compared to iOS, we've just need to design two different size, for iphone and ipad. Well, three if you count the new ipad with retina display.

Maybe I must learn more about responsive design or get to know more about layout design in Android development. But for lazy guy like me, who want to build something simple pretty fast, right now android is not my option. Hope android world will evolve and reduce the fragmentation problems.
